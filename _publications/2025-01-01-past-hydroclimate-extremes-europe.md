excerpt: 'Reconstructions of the Euro-Atlantic jet stream for every month over the past 600 years show that excursions of the jet often caused hydroclimatic extremes such as floods or drought in Central Europe. Though El Niño and volcanic eruptions exert an influence, most of the variation of the the Euro-Atlantic jet is unforced. Results also show that recent changes in the jet, though at time pronounced, are within the variability of the past 600 years.'
